# Overview

This is a very personal project to me, as I managed the recording of a live session for my band in the countryside of Hungary.

# My Role

- Editing
- Color Grading
- Songwriting / Composing
- Performing
- Producing
- Mixing / Mastering

# Process

I started the Perkupa band in 2021 after a creative retreat in the Hungarian countryside. In 2025 the band decided to have a Live Session, and I started to organize the crew around that. 

This was a project that combined songwriting, live performance, cinematography, recording techniques and mixing and mastering.

The crew worked with a lightweight, minimal equipment so that it wouldn't be intrusive to the performances but could still capture the most amount of sound. 

The recording session took place during a single day. We ended up recording 5 songs. Among the 5, 2 were written during a songwriting exercise the day before.

In term of recording, all of the instruments were tracked live with a combination of live monitoring and digital recording. The drums were captured with a 4 microphone setup (kick, snare, stereo overheads), the guitars, bass and vocals were monitored through an open PA but were capture through a digital interface.

The audio bleed that went into the drum microphones were handled with frequency overlap and AI stem separation for a more accurate mix.

After the crew handed over the files, I took over all of the post-production. I rerecorded vocals, mixed and mastered the audio, and color corrected the video (solved the problem of light imbalance between the recording area / background).

I handled the promotional side and uploading to Spotify / YouTube / Instagram.

# Results

The reels that promoted the track punched way above the follower count of the band. Nothing went viral, yet we had high view counts on all of these reels. 

The YouTube videos performed fairly well, as it earned the band about 1000 unique viewers and 1500 total views. For a band this size, these metrics are quite high.
