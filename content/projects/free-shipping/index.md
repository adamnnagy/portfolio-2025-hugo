# My role

I made this VR experience from scratch in Unreal Engine 4. I designed some models, outsourced some, I wrote the logic and interactions for the objects that are featured in the game.

{{% image src="images/free-shipping/freeshipping-07.jpg" alt="A screenshot from the VR experience" class="content-image" %}}

## Interactive exploration of racial biasing

I gamified the experience of unconscious/racial biasing. Inspired by games like "Papers Please", "Undertale", "Train", the player is doing a mundane job and has to rely on morally questionable biases to do well and earn points.

I collected data for the items online and from personal testimonials. This included incidents that affected individuals at airports, and people reported being subject to "random checks" and reoccurring stops by border control. In the testimonials I received, the ethnicity of the subject was usually a determining factor.

Ethnicities: 
- African
- Middle Eastern
- Caucasian
- Hispanic

I left off the East Asian community after running multiple test sessions beforehand. Level 3 posed a significant challenge for the players with the time constraint, and I decided to simplify the game, leaving a possibility for future 

## Core concept

I represented ethnicity with personal belongings and placed the player inside a storage facility. Their task was to sort through items coming from a conveyer belt and load them into trucks that were labelled according to major ethnicities. I designed boxes so that the items are easily identifiable by both images and text.

## Level progression

{{% image src="images/free-shipping/presentation/freeshipping-presentation-06.jpeg" alt="A screenshot from the VR experience" class="content-image" %}}

The levels progressed from simple to relatively complex through Level 1 to Level 3. The experience was designed so that every player were able to finish it, there was no action that resulted in a Game Over. 

## Scoreboard and scoring system

{{% image src="images/free-shipping/presentation/freeshipping-presentation-08.jpeg" alt="A screenshot from the VR experience" class="content-image" %}}

Excellence at the game was measured by points that the players scored, which appeared on the leaderboard.

## Sound

<!-- {{% image src="images/free-shipping/presentation/freeshipping-presentation-09.jpeg" alt="A screenshot from the VR experience" class="content-image" %}} -->

<!-- {{< audioplayer folder="free-shipping" >}} -->

The sound FX were an important part of the game. I recorded lines with Ruobing Zhao, a fellow student from China. Her accent and presence gave the game world an interesting edge. It served as a voice of God / Public Announcement narration that was took some of the complicity off of the player, giving the instructions. This was inspired by the [Stanford Prison Experiment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stanford_prison_experiment) and the [experiments of Stanley Milgram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milgram_experiment).

## Ending

{{% image src="images/free-shipping/presentation/freeshipping-presentation-07.jpeg" alt="A screenshot from the VR experience" class="content-image" %}}

The story ends with the player signing a non-disclosure agreement. This is how most cases of corporate ethical breaches are settled, so it seemed fitting to have that as an ending.

# Reception


The VR experience premiered at the NYU Capstone Festival and it met universal acclaim. Because of the high demand, I was asked to add extra viewing slots for people to see it, and I was invited to give a talk about it at a roundtable discussion and class presentations.
