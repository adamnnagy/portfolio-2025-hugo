# iGEM

iGEM is an international not-for-profit organization dedicated to advancing the field of synthetic biology to find solutions for a healthier, more resilient and sustainable world.

They organize:
* a competition between 450 academic teams
* education about synthetic biology
* science collaborative community

## Synthetic Biology?

Synthetic biology is adapting engineering principles to the field of biology. It's like playing LEGO with genes. The LEGO blocks - the genes - will eventually decide how the organisms will look like and how they are going to behave. 

# The Show videos

Script, Edit, Sound Design | Paris

Each year, a huge synthetic biology expo taking place in Paris, at the Porte de Versailles Expo Centre. It's called the Grand Jamboree. Each year, there's a big show - think videos, theatrics, even cold sparks amid the scientific conferences and presentations. There are over 4500 people who attend.

The videos are screened on an array of wide LED screen (6144px * 1024px), and then later they are adapted to smaller formats.

## The Super wide Village videos, opening videos

These videos is document synthetic biology projects that achieved success in environmental, technological or healthcare. 

They are the real hard hitters at the Grand Jamboree. They help unite the field - the technological aspects with the overarching goals of the program. 





# Live Show video inspired by the Olympics

2024 marked a big year in Paris. The city hosted the Olympic Games and the whole world was watching. iGEM decided to create its own version of the passing of the Olympic flame.

The Biobrick Trophy used for 18 years was filled up with the names of the previous winners. A new Biobrick Trophy was designed and it replaced the old Biobrick Trophy at the final day of the Grand Jamboree.

# Grand Jamboree Show Videos

**2024–2025 | Script, Edit, Sound Design**

Each year, [iGEM’s Grand Jamboree](https://jamboree.igem.org/) brings together more than 4,500 participants from around the world for a week-long conference and competition in Paris.

The show videos are central pieces of the live production experience. Screened on large-scale LED installations, they introduce key moments of the program, celebrate achievements in synthetic biology, and help create a cohesive visual identity for the event.

The content was originally designed for ultra-wide stage screens and later adapted into standard 16:9 formats for online release.

# About [iGEM](https://igem.org/)

iGEM is an international non-profit organization dedicated to advancing synthetic biology through education, research, and global collaboration.

The organization is best known for:

* organizing an international competition involving more than 450 academic teams,
* fostering interdisciplinary collaboration in synthetic biology,
* and supporting education and public engagement in emerging biotechnology.

---

# Production Context

Many of these projects were created for live presentation environments rather than traditional online distribution.

The Grand Jamboree show content was screened on ultra-wide LED displays inside the Paris Expo Porte de Versailles venue, requiring custom compositions, motion graphics workflows, and delivery formats optimized for large-scale live projection.

The challenge was not only technical, but narrative: translating complex scientific ideas into emotionally engaging stories for an international audience of students, researchers, industry professionals, and sponsors.
